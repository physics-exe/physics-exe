This site summarizes the day-ahead reefer-load forecasting pipeline built in hackathon_reefer_dl: a strong anchor forecast for the full window, a peak specialist for the hardest hours, and a selective handoff strategy that produced the current strongest public result in the repo.

Context View

Challenge Setup

Forecast hourly reefer electricity demand one day ahead, with a useful upper-risk estimate.

Objective

Predict the terminal's aggregate reefer power demand for every released public timestamp.

The target is not a single container. It is the combined hourly load of all plugged-in reefer containers, which means the model has to capture both the changing container mix and the cooling conditions across the yard.

    Score Function

    0.5 × MAE_all + 0.3 × MAE_peak + 0.2 × pinball_p90

    The scoring setup punishes missed peaks, not just average error. That is why the solution uses both a point forecast and a calibrated p90 upper band instead of treating uncertainty as an afterthought.

    Everything shown here is labeled clearly as public-window performance. The hidden private rerun was reserved for the organizers.

    Proxy Study

    Noisy forecast weather still keeps most of the predictive temperature signal.

    The participant package gives us realized weather, not archived forecasts. To approximate a realistic day-ahead setup, we take the existing weather series, inject controlled noise, and treat the result as a synthetic 24-hour forecast available earlier in time.

      Why It Matters

        Winning Recipe

        Best public result = strong anchor forecast plus a targeted tail specialist.

        The public score improved materially when the system stopped treating every hour the same. The anchor model handles the overall curve, then only the highest-risk hours are partially replaced by the specialist that is better at the extreme tail.
