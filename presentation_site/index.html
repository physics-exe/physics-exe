Challenge Setup

Forecast hourly reefer electricity demand one day ahead, with a useful upper-risk estimate.

Objective

Predict the terminal's aggregate reefer power demand for every released public timestamp.

The target is not a single container. It is the combined hourly load of all plugged-in reefer containers, which means the model has to capture both the changing container mix and the cooling conditions across the yard.

    Score Function

    0.5 × MAE_all + 0.3 × MAE_peak + 0.2 × pinball_p90

    The scoring setup punishes missed peaks, not just average error. That is why the solution uses both a point forecast and a calibrated p90 upper band instead of treating uncertainty as an afterthought.

    Everything shown here is labeled clearly as public-window performance. The hidden private rerun was reserved for the organizers.

    The model predicts a correction on top of a strong day-ahead baseline instead of trying to invent the full load curve from scratch.

    Winning Recipe

    Best public result = raw MLP output plus reproducible post-processing.

    The public score improved materially when the model output was blended with the lag-24 baseline according to low, medium, and high load terciles. That kept the method simple, explainable, and well aligned with the challenge metric.
